# Ought Statement

## The product must make possible

- A family can decide whether the property fits their stay within 30 seconds.
- An event organizer can understand whether the compound supports their gathering.
- A local guest can message on WhatsApp with date, pax, and purpose already structured.
- An international or outstation guest can understand location, safety, facilities, rules, and contact path.
- The public site can later connect to owner admin, booking records, payment, calendar, analytics, and legal adapters.
- Google, AI search, and social platforms can parse real property facts.

## The product must prevent

- Vague luxury claims without proof.
- Guests asking the same basic questions repeatedly.
- Wrong-fit bookings caused by unclear pax, rules, parking, event capacity, pool rules, or layout.
- A beautiful but slow, hard-to-update, weak-on-search website.
- F&B-template gravity controlling a homestay product.
- Hidden operational complexity being exposed too early to guests.

## The product must reveal

- Who the property is best for.
- Which occasions are suitable.
- How many people can sleep, gather, park, swim, cook, and relax.
- Where the pool, house, guest house, gazebo, parking, and open areas are.
- What proof supports each claim.
- What action the visitor should take next.

## The product must preserve

- Direct booking ownership.
- Low maintenance burden.
- Mobile-first performance.
- Future readiness for availability, booking, payment, CRM, analytics, and operator control.
